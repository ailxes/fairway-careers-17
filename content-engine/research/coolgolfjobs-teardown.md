# @coolgolfjobs Growth Teardown

**Date:** 2026-07-22 · **Method:** Full 26-post scrape (Apify), whisper transcripts of the 9 earliest videos with audio, comment mining, cross-referenced against `instagram-content-research/master_script_framework.md`.

## The headline numbers

- Account launched **2026-05-08**. First "Five Coolest Jobs" video **2026-06-01**.
- Creator states on camera (6/05): "Monday we had 200 followers, today we have 3,500." That is **17x in one week** off the format's first two videos.
- **14,598 followers by 2026-07-22** on just 26 posts. The format, not volume, drives growth.
- Viral spikes: 6/05 five-jobs (143k plays, 4,608 likes), 6/01 first five-jobs (121k, 3,310), 6/18 Roger Steele career story (81k, 4,679 likes, 167 comments — highest engagement rate), 7/16 Augusta single-job (49k).
- Baseline non-format / personal posts: 8–16k plays. **The flagship format outperforms the personal content ~10x.**
- Monetized fast: "Segment is brought to you by Big Blade Putters" by 6/08 — sponsor slot sold within 5 weeks of launch.

## The "Five Coolest Jobs" formula (from transcripts)

1. **Ritual opener tied to day of week.** "It's Monday, you know what that means." / "It's Friday, I've got a tee time, but before that..." Monday + Friday cadence = appointment content. Comment proof: "Just casually here for this every week."
2. **Countdown 1→5, winner withheld.** #5 is crowned "the coolest job in golf this week." Countable structure + withheld winner = the loop stays open to the last second (passes the master framework's line-one gate).
3. **Per-job beat (~15–20s): brand name + title, then exactly ONE flavor device:**
   - Relatable joke: "Senior Software Engineer for GolfNow — become the COO of your dad's tee times."
   - Transferable-skills push: "Why the hell would I want to be an accountant for a health company when I could be an accountant for Ping?"
   - Insider knowledge: "If you ever wondered how that guy got into the hospitality booth at a PGA Tour stop — he's probably in advertising."
   - Aspirational gatekeeping: "Ranger/Starter at Bandon Dunes — this one's only for the dudes that are chill."
4. **Recurring mantras as brand identity** (repeated verbatim across videos, on purpose — he says "a trip that I'm gonna hit every week"):
   - "Take your boring job, get a new job in golf."
   - "Retail is a real job. Do not sleep on retail."
5. **Community as content engine:** DM-sourced jobs ("one of you sent me a DM at 4am" → emergency-correspondent post), audience challenged to fill roles ("it would make me smitten if we could find that person and get them employed"), follower-count gratitude said aloud.
6. **Calendar theming:** Father's Day edition (jobs → employee discounts → gifts), Nelly Korda win → LPGA edition, hometown Disney job.
7. **Light reach CTA, always the same:** "All the job listings are in my bio. See you Monday."
8. **Each job unit is independently sendable.** He models sharing on camera: "I've already sent this to 20 people." Every ~20s beat resets retention — that's why 90–130s runtimes survive despite the 35–50s teaching-format rule; a countdown is 5 complete short units, not one long lesson.

## Second format: the career-story breakdown (highest engagement of all)

6/18 Roger Steele: "From civil engineer to golf media — how Roger Steele became successful, in 60 seconds." Timeline structure (2011 → 2025), checkable milestones, third-party proof (TGL host, Lululemon signing). **Audience-requested** ("I've gotten a lot of DMs asking for jobs where they interview people"). 4,679 likes / 167 comments on 81k plays — the engagement-rate king. This validates a "how X got their cool golf job" slot.

## Why the account grows (mechanism, not vibes)

1. **Weekly appointment ritual** → repeat viewers → follows.
2. **Share-shaped units** → each job beat is a complete, sendable gift ("this job IS you").
3. **Two-sided marketplace in comments** → employers post "we're hiring, DM me" (top comment 17♥: "I'm hiring videographers & editors 👀"). Supply attracts demand attracts supply.
4. **Brand tags** → tagged employers engage and reshare ("Thanks for the @callawaygolf shout out!").
5. **Identity flag** → "escape your boring job into golf" is a belief people follow to keep believing.
6. **Named, checkable proof** → real brands, real listings, verifiable numbers ("only 208 people on LinkedIn have applied").

## Where the account is weak (TeeUpJobs' opening)

1. **Cadence broke.** Gap 7/10 → 7/15 ("IM BACK"), captions turned personal, non-format posts sit at 8–12k plays vs 120k+ peaks. Consistency is the moat he's abandoning.
2. **No owned platform.** Bio links to a raw Google Doc. No newsletter, no site, no search, no archive. Top comment on his own post: "Applying on LinkedIn sucks" — a pain he surfaces but does not solve. TeeUpJobs solves it (searchable board + newsletter capture + monetization tables already built).
3. **Unserved demand sitting in his comments:** UK jobs, women's audience ("*Dudes* or maybe hear me out… dudettes"), regional editions ("DMs flooded with people asking about region-specific jobs").
4. **Single-channel risk.** All audience on IG; no email. Every follower is rented.

## Directives for the TeeUpJobs generator

- Flagship: **Five Coolest Jobs** countdown, Mon + Fri feel, winner withheld to the end, one flavor device per job, all listings CTA → teeupjobs.com.
- Slot 2: **career-story breakdown** (timeline + checkable milestones) — highest engagement format.
- Slot 3 rotation: no-experience jobs (DB tag exists), $80k+ jobs (comp columns), employer spotlight, themed editions on calendar hooks.
- Keep the mantra device: TeeUpJobs needs 1–2 recurring lines said every video, verbatim.
- Tag every employer featured. Invite employers to comment openings.
- Script specs from master framework: hook withholds or is checkable, one keyword in supporting hook not line one, ~3.5 wps, every on-screen line readable <3s, caption always, no em dashes, no same-day duplicates.
- Countdown exception to the 35–50s rule: 5 units × ~15–20s ≈ 75–100s is fine; each unit must be complete and sendable on its own.
